## Process overview

### Initiation
Project initiation validates a new idea before delivery work begins. Teams align on the business need, success metrics, stakeholders, timeline, early risks, and a go/no-go decision for moving into planning.

### Planning
Planning turns an approved initiative into an actionable backlog and release plan. The team defines shippable increments, acceptance criteria, estimates, dependencies, risks, milestones, and the Definition of Done.

### Execution & Tracking
Execution focuses on daily delivery, visibility, and quality. OctoAcme uses regular team rhythms, project board workflows, small pull requests, CI checks, demos, and metrics to track progress and escalate blockers quickly.

### Risk Management & Communication
Risk management and communication keep stakeholders aligned while reducing delivery surprises. Risks are captured in a register, reviewed throughout the project lifecycle, and paired with regular status updates and clear escalation paths.

### Release & Deployment
Release and deployment standardize how features move to production. The process emphasizes acceptance criteria completion, passing CI and security scans, release notes, smoke tests, rollout verification, and rollback readiness.

### Retrospective & Continuous Improvement
Retrospectives help the team capture lessons learned after sprints, releases, milestones, and incidents. The goal is to turn feedback into a small set of owned action items that are tracked to completion and measured for impact.

### Roles & Personas
OctoAcme project work is organized around clear roles and personas. Developers build and test solutions, Product Managers define value and priorities, and Project Managers coordinate timelines, risks, documentation, and stakeholder communication.
